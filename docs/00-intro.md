# THE AGENTIC ARCHITECTURE FRAMEWORK V1.3

## Note from the AFF Working Group Chair, Dave Maddock

I'm not writing this as a definitive expert or as the final word on agentic systems. I'm writing it as a practitioner who has spent years operating at the boundary between conceptual architecture and production reality—first as an enterprise cloud architect, and more recently as an AI solution architect. My working belief is simple: when systems evolve faster than our collective understanding, we need frameworks—conceptual scaffolding that helps teams reason, design, and govern effectively, even when the underlying technology is moving beneath our feet.

This document is the accumulation of ideas, patterns, and controls I've captured while designing and delivering large-scale systems across enterprise environments, and then stress-testing those same principles against the distinct failure modes of AI and agents. Building the WrangleAI platform has accelerated that learning curve. WrangleAI exists to help organisations govern and optimise AI usage in practice, and developing it has forced me to engage these topics routinely—both in our own engineering decisions and through repeated client conversations where cost, security, reliability, and operational control stop being theoretical and become immediate.

The future is already here, and the burden is now on us to keep up. Please take this for what it is: a Version 1 framework intended to help you deliver better agents, a structure to support clearer thinking, safer design, and more repeatable outcomes. Feedback, challenge, and counterexamples are not just welcome; they're necessary. In this space, the moment something is written, it starts to go out of date. My hope is that this artifact still earns its place as a stable reference point: a way to learn, compare approaches, and introduce order into the chaos of the fast-moving agentic landscape.

To get to the moon, first you have to get out of bed. This is v1.

Dave
